Introduction

Imagine that one day you decide to place all your savings under your mattress. Yes you forget this money for a year, it does will bring you nothing. You will find exactly the same amount. Now imagine that you have instead used that money to buy assets financial, such as stocks or raw materials. Instead of being at a standstill, your money would have benefited from a potential much greater growth because these actions or raw materials are likely to gain value. But conversely, they can also lose value. To invest in the financial markets it is therefore important to choose assets likely to evolve in the direction of your anticipation and then finding the ideal balance between risk of loss and potential for gain. As we will see, trading can, if it is applied in a prudent and reasoned manner earn more than the interest rates charged by the banks
